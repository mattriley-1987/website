Club Racing

Weston SC Racing Programme can be found on the Programme page.

Club Racing at Weston is all year round subject to tides (apart from January when Training and Work Party Duty takes place)

The Sailing Calender Splits into the following main blocks of activity

  • Freezer Series (Feb – Apr)
  • Spring Series   (Apr – Jun)
  • Easter Grand Slam (Easter Weekend)
  • Evening Series (May – Aug)
  • Summer Series (Jun – Aug)
  • Autumn Series (Sep – Sep)
  • Frostbite Series (Oct – Dec)

Additionally held at intervals throughout the year are a variety of races from Cup Weekends, Pursuit races, Youth Regatta, Long distance raids and more.

Dear member,

Following a pre-release of the RYA 2014 PY numbers, the WSC Sailing Committee have convened to discuss the impact of the proposed PY changes to our existing racing Class splits. We have also taken this opportunity to consider feedback from members during the 2013 season about our racing Class splits. Some small changes are now being made – effective immediate – however we have kept these to a minimum.

The revised WSC Class splits are outlined below and will now be in operation for 2014, effective immediately. There is an accompanying commentary where appropriate, explaining the reasons behind any change.

WSC Classes for 2014:

FAST HANDICAP  - (Fleet no. 1) - PY 945 and faster
(Catamarans without spinnakers may choose to race in the Medium fleet, for the duration of a series)

MEDIUM ASYMMETRIC - (Fleet no. 2)  - PY 946 – 1100      
(Depending on turnout, this fleet may share a course and start with FAST HANDICAP)
HANDICAP  (COMMENT – Minor adjustment of fast PY cut-off to reflect RYA PY changes. Extension of slow PY cut off to accommodate Laser 2000 and other Asymm dinghies)

MEDIUM HANDICAP - (Fleet no. 3) - PY 946 – 1023      
(Depending on turnout, may share course and start with SLOW. )
(COMMENT -  Band tightened to exclude most non trapeze single handers, who prefer to sail in the SLOW fleet)

SLOW - (Fleet no. 4) - PY 1024 and slower.  
(NOTE: Boats with a PY 1024- 1100 with an asymmetric spinnaker sail in Medium Asymmetric)

(COMMENT – Band widened to accommodate some dinghies from Medium Handicap fleet, which is mainly Contenders)

Race Officers – please note the above guidance for combining starts and courses where appropriate – this is essential to providing the optimal racing experience for competitors.

Courses

General Courses set, will be Windward Leeward , Trapezoidal, or Triangle at the request of Class Fleet Captains. Marker Buoys and some fixed marks will be used. Club starts will be from a shore based race box using a transit and outer distance inflatable mark. Class starts may be combined by the Race Officer for WSC club racing, this will be advised on the course board if so.
