Easter Grand Slam 2013

Weston Sailing Club

Report:

Weston Sailing Club format for the Grand Slam changed to a three day event this year with an optional Pursuit Race on the Monday. The race format was three races back to back each day, so a nine race regatta. This proved to be challenge as the weather was the coldest Easter Weston has ever experienced, but on the plus side it was dry and no snow. The Race officer Matt Humphreys had a offshore breeze to deal with and running the event from the shore based race box, this would mean a American Cup reaching start to the turning buoy and then downwind. Further to this we had Five Fleets , Catamaran, Fast, (Fast Asy), Medium, (Fast Non Asy), Slow Medium, (Slow Ays, using the RS400 as the cut off), Slow,(slow Non asy). All using the lastest 2013 RYA Handicaps. The Slow Medium fleet proved to be a success with very close racing.

Conditions were dry, wind NE variable 8 knots , gusts to 20 knots, this set a pattern for the weekend racing.Â  Fifty boats turned up in the cold looking for a competitive race. In the Cat fleet Grant Piggott and Simon Farren set the pace and soon went on to set a cap between them in the F18 and the rest of the fleet. The real battle would be for second and third places between the F18s and a well-sailed Unicorn of Owen Cox from Weston SC. In the fast fleet the 49er Ron Price and Amy Cartwright from Weston SC would have a competitive race with the Cherub Paul and Peter Jenkins . A couple moths also showed there pace but the wind had many holes in it and proved there downfall as they dropped off the foils. Matthew Holden sailing a RS600 could have set the pace but missed Friday racing, however he was one of only two visitors who stayed for the Pursuit Race and was rewarded with the win. The Medium fleet showed the strong Weston Contender fleet would be the ones to beat with Robert Angus and Martin Frary setting the pace. Slow medium would prove to be the most competitive fleet overall with a matter of seconds meaning finishing first or eight. A tight battle ended up with the RS200 Richard and Luke Bailey ending up in the Chocolates with R100 Steven Lee following closely and Weston’s Young guns RS200 Amber and Jamie Brown taking third.

Bank sails provided the Prizes, many thanks to them and all the competitors who braved the condition to make a great three days racing.
