Warm-Up Open Jan 2013

Weston Warm Up 2013

First sail of the New Year for many saw contrasts of weather conditions. First day of the Open held from Weston Sailing Club saw a light SW breeze , overcast but not too cold for January, on the Sunday it blow up starting F4 to F6 and easing for the first race to F4 to F5 but the gust top out at 32 Knots. However all four races were held , the format is two races a day with a break to come ashore for a well earned hot drink, one discard would be available. The Classes were Asymmetric and Asymmetric Catamarans sailing a windward leeward in class one. Class two would Fast PY cut 1050. Class three Slow higher than 1050.

We saw a varied fleet the strongest being the 49er with five boats and Contender next then a mix of Laser Rigs. This made for some interesting PY racing, the Race Officer decided on three courses but two starts to make the start line a little more interesting for the sailors.

Day One

In the First race on Saturday the breeze was light 5 – 10 knots which made it difficult for the F18 of Grant and Adam to hold off the 49ers. Ron and Amy took the first race in the 49er. The second race saw just a touch more breeze and Grant and Adam won that one.

In class two we saw Mike Murley show good spped in the light breeze and taking the two bullets with Rob Jones in the Blaze showing some good pace. In class three Olly and Son Ben took both races in the Vibe X, Sam Woods would be chasing in the Laser.

Day Two

Breeze on F4 to F6 would see a couple drop out Olly and Ben would pull out putting pay to there chances of a pot. Amy and Ron saw boat breaking weather and pull out. Class one now proved the F18 rules the waves in a blow and saw off the 49ers to win overall, Class two saw Rob Jones in the Blaze winning by a couple of seconds from Rob Angus in the Contender, Mike Murley did not compete so left the door open. Class three saw a tight battle between the Laser of Sam Woods and the well sailed GP14 of Chris and Cathy Hawkes visiting from Poole YC. Sam would go on to win.
