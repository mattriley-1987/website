REPORT

ASYMMETRIC OPEN
From Saturday 13th July to Sunday 14th July 2013
 Fast & Medium Slow

Report:

Weston Sailing Club format for the Asymmetric Open changed to include two fleets the split was Fast PY 946 and faster and Slow Medium PY 947 and slower. This put the RS400 into the slow medium fleet, with the RS 100.

The weather forecast was predicting light winds with a possible sea breeze, however the first day turned out the sea breeze was showing at bramble met in the Solent only three miles down the Southampton water but would not find its way down to us. Racing was canned for Saturday. BBQ and Bowman’s Ale was the way to finish a glorious sunny evening.

Sunday once again started with Hot! Hot! Sunshine and a promise of sea breeze, this time it did not let use down. Perfect wind of 12 to 15 Knots saw the fleet complete some champagne sailing.  Fast fleet was dominated by the South African Weston SC members in the 49 er with straight bullets to win, with the visiting ISO fleet mixing it up on PY to take second John Gill & Juliet Daniels and third Colin Snook &Alison Revitt. Slow Medium fleet saw the RS100 of Weston SC, Stephen Lee winning with RS 200 of Weston SC Tim Golding and Josie Bell taking second and RS 100 Warash SC  Kieron Holt third.
